## When to use which level

`h1` is the **page head** — one per surface, paired with the route's
breadcrumb. The eyebrow names the data source or context (`Sources · live
now`, `run · classify-prod`); the title names the screen.

`h2` is the **section head** inside a panel — groups of rows or controls
that ladder under the page head. The eyebrow tags the group (`Schedule ·
cron`, `Filters · 3 active`); the title carries the noun phrase.

`h3` is the **inspector / panel inner head**. Use it when an `h2` already
owns the panel and you need a tighter sub-stop — typically inside a card,
a right-rail inspector, or a settings sub-block.

The eyebrow is optional. Drop it for the bare title when the surrounding
chrome (a breadcrumb, a chip strip, a section eyebrow already on the
page) is doing the labeling work.
