## The four-band model

Every list row is in exactly one of four bands at any moment: **base**, **zebra**, **hover**,
**selected**. Each band picks a different hue axis so they never collide. **Zebra** is
structural — a cool ink-step that carries scan rhythm. **Hover** is transient — a soft tint of
the active blue that previews the eventual selection. **Selected** is committed — saturated
blue with white text. **Base** is "this row is here," equal to the panel `--bg`.

Rows are not flush. A `gap: 2px` between every row lets the panel `--bg` show through, which is
what makes the alternating zebra fills tile rather than smear and lets a hovered or selected
row read as a discrete tile lifting out of the list — without any per-row border.
